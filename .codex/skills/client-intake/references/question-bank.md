# Client Intake Question Bank

Use this bank to ask only the questions that materially change scope, cost, timeline, or solution shape.

## Questioning Principles

- Ask the minimum number of questions needed to move forward.
- Prefer grouped questions over one-off back-and-forth.
- Separate must-answer questions from nice-to-know questions.
- If the brief is very raw, start with the core pack only.
- If the brief is already semi-clear, skip to the relevant conditional pack.

## Question Order

1. Project objective
2. Target user or stakeholder
3. Deliverables
4. Deadline and urgency
5. Budget or effort constraints
6. Platform or environment
7. Integrations and dependencies
8. Approval and sign-off
9. Risks, exclusions, and support expectations

## Core Pack

Ask these first when the brief is raw or incomplete:

- What problem are we solving?
- What is the final outcome you want?
- Who is the main user or buyer?
- What does success look like?
- When do you need this delivered?
- Is there a budget or effort cap?
- Who makes the final decision?

## Scope Pack

Use when the main question is what will actually be built:

- What must be included in version 1?
- What should be excluded for now?
- Which features are must-have versus nice-to-have?
- Are there any examples of similar products you want to follow?
- What would make you say the project is complete?

## Audience And Workflow Pack

Use when the user journey is not clear:

- Who will use this day to day?
- What steps do they take now?
- What step should be faster or easier after this project?
- Are there multiple user roles?
- Does anyone need admin or management access?

## Timeline Pack

Use when schedule affects scope or sequencing:

- Is there a hard deadline or flexible target date?
- Is the deadline tied to a launch, event, or internal review?
- What happens if the project ships in phases?
- Are there milestone dates we must hit?
- Is there a review window or waiting period after delivery?

## Budget Pack

Use when cost or effort is constrained:

- Do you have a target budget range?
- Is the budget fixed or flexible?
- Would you prefer a smaller first phase or a larger single delivery?
- Is there room for follow-up work later?
- Should we optimize for speed, cost, or quality first?

## Platform And Tech Pack

Use when the delivery surface matters:

- Is this web, mobile, desktop, API, internal tool, or automation?
- Do you already have a stack or hosting preference?
- Should this fit into an existing codebase or start fresh?
- Are there browser, device, or OS requirements?
- Is there an existing domain, cloud, or account setup?

## Design And Brand Pack

Use when the user will judge visual polish:

- Do you already have a brand guide or design system?
- Are there reference sites you like or dislike?
- Should the style feel conservative, premium, playful, or minimal?
- Do you need copywriting, visuals, or only implementation?
- Are there approved logos, colors, or fonts?

## Content Pack

Use when text, media, or documents are part of delivery:

- Do you already have all required content?
- Who will provide copy, images, and legal text?
- Are there missing assets that could delay the project?
- Do you need migration of existing content?
- Should we prepare placeholder content or wait for final assets?

## Integration Pack

Use when the project touches external systems:

- Does this need payment, email, auth, CRM, ERP, maps, or analytics?
- Which external systems must it connect to?
- Do you already have API keys or access?
- Is there a preferred integration approach?
- Are third-party costs or rate limits a concern?

## Data And Migration Pack

Use when existing data is involved:

- Is there old data that needs to be imported?
- What format is the source data in?
- How much data is there?
- Does any data need cleaning or mapping?
- Who owns verification after migration?

## Access And Approval Pack

Use when delivery depends on people or permissions:

- Who is the main contact?
- Who approves scope and final delivery?
- Who can provide access to required accounts?
- Are there any legal, compliance, or procurement steps?
- Do you want updates daily, weekly, or at milestones?

## Security And Compliance Pack

Use when sensitive data or production access is involved:

- Does this handle personal, financial, or internal data?
- Are there security, privacy, or compliance requirements?
- Do we need role-based access or audit logs?
- Are there regions or data residency constraints?
- Is there a review process before go-live?

## Launch And Support Pack

Use when handoff and post-launch matter:

- What counts as done and ready to launch?
- Do you need demo, staging, or production environments?
- Who handles support after delivery?
- Do you want documentation or a handover checklist?
- Are bug fixes or post-launch changes included?

## Fast Triage Pack

If the brief is very rough, ask these 5 first:

- What is the goal?
- Who is it for?
- What do you want delivered?
- When do you need it?
- Is there a budget or scope limit?

## Escalation Triggers

Stop intake and escalate if the answer reveals:

- unclear owner or approver
- hard deadline with broad scope
- payment, auth, or production data risk
- missing access that blocks delivery
- major ambiguity in what success means
