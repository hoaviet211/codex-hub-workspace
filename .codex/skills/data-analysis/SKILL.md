# Data Analysis

## Overview

Use this skill to convert raw data, metrics, or behavioral patterns into decisions. Focus on what the data actually supports, what it does not support, and what action should follow.

## Workflow

1. Define the question, target metric, and business or operational outcome.
2. Identify the data source, grain, time window, and known quality limits.
3. Analyze trends, segments, funnels, cohorts, or anomalies relevant to the question.
4. Translate findings into insight, hypothesis, or algorithm direction.
5. Recommend the next experiment, automation, or optimization step.

## Focus Areas

- Clarify metric definitions before drawing conclusions.
- Separate correlation from likely causation.
- Segment by user type, channel, time, or workflow step when averages hide behavior.
- Use behavioral patterns to suggest rules, heuristics, ranking logic, or workflow changes.
- Call out missing data, biased samples, and instrumentation gaps.
- Optimize for decisions the client can actually act on.

## Output Rules

- State the business question and answer it directly.
- Quantify findings where the data supports quantification.
- Distinguish observed fact, inference, and recommendation.
- End with concrete optimization actions or algorithm hypotheses.
